What is PII (Personally Identifiable Information) in India?

Personally Identifiable Information (PII) refers to any data that can directly or indirectly identify an individual. In India, this includes:

  • Direct identifiers: Name, phone number, email, residential address, Aadhaar, PAN, passport details.
  • Sensitive personal data: Financial info, health records, biometrics, passwords, official IDs.
  • Indirect identifiers: IP address, location data, device IDs when combined with other information.

Why is Protecting PII Important?

  • Privacy & Trust: Builds user trust by safeguarding information.
  • Legal Compliance: Aligns with Indian data protection laws, avoiding penalties.
  • Cybersecurity: Prevents misuse in identity theft, fraud, and phishing.
  • User Control: Gives individuals control over their data.
